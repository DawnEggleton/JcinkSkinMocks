'We Need Supersoldiers': Quantum Biomedical Secures Military Contract

Written by: Timothy Mayer | September 13, 2106

Quantum Biomedical has managed to impress Washington: enough that the company has secured a coveted military contract. The company, with origins in Davenport, Maine, just recently passed FDA approvals with their latest in biomodification regimens, known only as the Metahuman Serum. The serum has undergone extensive testing and has been proven a safe method of altering human DNA to acquire powers akin to that of the newly discovered metahumans. While the company has stated vying for a contract since before human testing began, they claim it was the Whitehouse that approached them.

"We're just as surprised as you, but the President said it best," claims Janus Project lead scientist. "The world is on edge and we need a way to fight back against the potential of a metahuman threat. We need supersoldiers."

The Whitehouse has yet to provide an official comment, but the Secretary of Defense confirmed that the contract was sought by the government and not the other way around.

Quantum Bio claims that, while they don't need the money to keep the program going, they appreciate the support and have opted to put 20% of the government's payout back into the community that has stood by them throughout the economic crash and enabled them to keep the lights on long enough to discover their serum. They are donating half of the money directly to the university, funding a new biochemistry lab building's construction, and the remaining half has gone to the mayor's office to be used for revitalization of the city.

The first soldiers to be altered will undergo their first treatment before the end of the month. The government hopes to have the first troop of supersoldiers ready to fight as early as mid-October.

Welcome to Play God! We're so excited to meet you - but first, a few ground rules to get us started! These should all be pretty straightforward - certainly nothing you haven't seen before!
behaviour
  • There is one big thing of note: We have a zero-tolerance policy for bullying, harassment, intimidation, and any form of bigotry. While we know and understand that some of us will come from a place of privilege and may misspeak or have misunderstandings about the lived experiences of our fellow writers, we insist that you approach everyone with kindness, understanding, and a desire to know and do better.
  • If you feel unsafe due to the actions or words of any other member, please bring it up immediately to the staff. We have a ticketing system in our Discord server which will allow you to get all of us in one spot at once without anyone else knowing you've done so. We encourage you to use it. If your issue is with a member of staff, take it to a staff member you trust in private messages on-site or on Discord. We will take any and all reports of this manner very seriously, we promise.
registration
  • Play God has a 3-3-3 rating per rpgrating.com. As such, we insist that you are 18+ upon joining and that all non-NPC characters are also 18+.
  • Speaking of characters, each character should have their own account - but so should you! We use OOC accounts here and they double as your member directory entry, so make sure to link your character accounts to your member account and not the other way around!
  • Now about names- we accept duplicates! Just do your best not to have a duplicate of a full name already in play. Two Johns is fine, two John Smiths is not! Exceptions are different spellings (Sara vs Sarah) or instances where you register them with their first name as a common nickname (e.g., having Alexandra Smith registered as Alex Smith is fine, even if there's already an Alexandra Smith on the board)!
  • We don't currently have a character cap- but this can change. We'd rather trust you to moderate your own ability to keep up... but we will step in if we have to.
faces
  • We accept real-life face claims only, and the images must be of good quality. So long as the face you intend to use has enough good quality images and has not expressly requested to not be used in roleplay, you're good to go! We won't restrict you any further than that.
  • As with characters and members, we ask that all faces also be 18+.
  • We don't believe in plus or minus rules here - so just be reasonable! If they can pass for the age you're stating (or if their powers account for the age/visual difference), you're gold!
  • We think this goes without saying - but no duplicate faces unless they're identical siblings.
content
  • All mature threads must be marked as such in the title, description, or using our post tags. Additionally, all triggering content must be warned at the start of the thread. If the subject is only in a handful of posts, please also leave a warning at the top of those posts. Otherwise, the warning at the start of the thread will suffice. With that said, so long as it's tagged appropriately and all involved in the writing are comfortable with the subject(s), we're not likely to restrict the large majority of subjects.
  • As for Discord, please make sure triggering content remains in the #trigger-warning channel. If it's heavy, please also spoiler it. Not sure how to spoiler something? Tag a staff member and ask!
applications
  • We use profile applications here - and we've put an awful lot of work into them to make them as functional and easy for you as possible. If you're on a new account, it should show you the fields you need to fill out based on what account type you chose in registration - which means, no scrolling for ages to reach the save button on member accounts! There are also click and drag sliders where appropriate and you can find tags that work in the main profile in our general use coding thread.
  • Although the fields won't kick back an error if you leave them blank (good for working in stages!), we ask that unless a field is marked optional, you fill out everything in the format asked. We tried to make it as painless and quick as possible, we promise! We aren't looking for novels - just enough to plot with!
  • That said - these fields can change. We know that characters are fluid creations and sometimes you learn something new once they're already accepted - and that's fine! If it's a major change, please just run it by a staff member first. Otherwise, pop it in/update the content and that's that! In fact, we actually insist you do this at the very least for the Relationships part of the profile!
  • Our member groups are based on where your characters live - but characters move, so what then? Pop on over to our update claims form and let us know! Simple as that.
  • Just so you're prepped: we use 4 images, but they all resize and crop to fit without distortion! One is a small square and is uploaded into your avatar settings. We suggest a minimum of 200x200. The others are in your profile fields and the recommended minimum sizes are in the field descriptions - but for convenience, they are 250x450, 450x700, and 250x120.
  • We do have a power limit for metahuman characters — no more than three. That said, if you're struggling to make powers fit in this limit, feel free to reach out to the staff! We may be able to help you! In terms of what powers are available, the answer is simple: anything is allowed with appropriate limitations in place. Sure, your character can have some level of omniscience... but there has to be drawbacks that balance it out. We're always willing if you want to run ideas by us to see if they'd be acceptable!
posting & activity
  • We ask that you timestamp all posts, even if it's vaguely ('mid-april 2021'). We have a code in place that will stylize this in topic descriptions, so please use the following format at the beginning of your topic descriptions: [date here]
  • The square brackets will work regardless of the content inside them, so you can use this for additional tags as well if you'd like.
  • No word counts here - we don't believe in those either. That said, be respectful. If your partner is struggling, try to give them a little more to work with. If you're struggling, let your partner know!
  • We expect one post per member per month or one post per month per major canon character, whichever is more (e.g., your canon character post(s) will count to keep your non-canons safe). Major canon characters are denoted with an asterisk (*) in the canon list. Posts must be in-character and not comms to count. Brand new characters/members are safe until the following month. There may be other requirements at various activity checks, but we'll do our best to keep them non-strenuous and solely for the purpose of plot progression - a check-in, if you will, and nothing more.
  • We have a tracking sheet that we try to update periodically so you can tell if you're safe or not, but sometimes it will fall behind. We double-check anyone in the red before acting on it, though, promise! We'll list either you individually (if you play no canon characters) or each of your canons, so please don't expect all your characters to be present on the sheet.
  • We may run monthly sweeps, but we're not going to be hard-asses about it. If life's taken over and you let us know you get a pass. We don't need a reason either - but please, be honest. If you're not actually going to be back in a week, don't tell us you will be. It's okay if you don't have a specific timeline - we can work with you to figure something out.
  • If you don't let us know, we have to assume you're struggling or you're gone. We'll reach out, but if it's repetitive, we can't promise anything. This is a hobby, but it is a collaborative hobby. Characters deemed inactive will be sorted into an inactive group and their posts/threads will be moved to archives. Inactive accounts display as member profiles to guests, so you'll need to log in to grab your info back - but we won't delete it without ample warning (> 1 month, via email and discord server).
  • Got caught in an activity check? You can still come back from it! Your first character re-sorting is automatic; just fill out the sorting form as you did initially and we'll get it it in the same period of time. After this, though, you're going to have to work a little more to prove you're capable of handling the load you've placed on yourself: for each additional character you want to move back to 'active' status, you'll need to make sure all of your currently active characters have had a post in the past week, based of when you fill out the sorting form again. For example, you have Mary, John, and Jane as characters. You get Mary re-sorted and post with her and, three days later, you ask to re-sort John. This is fine and we'll re-sort John for you. The next day, you request sorting for Jane - but you've not posted with Mary or John since John was re-sorted. Jane would be denied on this basis. Once you posted with Mary and John once each within the same week, you could then request that Jane be re-sorted. This is applicable to character in the inactive group, not new characters. That said, if we notice you getting caught in sweeps and just making new characters to avoid this, we will make an exception.
  • Need to go away for an extended period of time? Ask us about our hiatus option. It might be just what you need!
Central Davenport
Located just North of the river, the opulence of River's Edge bleeds over into the southern edge of this district. Featuring the city's university, business district, and a few smaller parks, it's an over all pleasant part of Davenport, regardless of the fact its in-land. It's primarily inhabited by the middle to middle-upper class as well as students. The streets are kept tidy and many are tree-lined with young oaks and maples.
Coastline
While the views of the Atlantic make the Coastline an appealing location, it's been relegated into two very distinct portions: the northern coastline, found on the north side of Old Town, and the much longer Southern strip. With the vast difference in community culture between the two, the wide range of socioeconomic classes is no surprise. Those in the north tend towards the lower or lower-middle class, while those in the south do better with the tourists and frequently manage to be steadily lower-middle class with a handful of lucky souls doing even better.
Old Town
The heart of Old Davenport, Old Town hasn't aged well. It maintains some of the original buildings along Main Street, but the shiny new piers south of it drew the tourists away, along with what cruise ships docked here at all. The drop in business led to struggles in the region and it has since become a shadow of what it was. Filled with criminals and the working poor, the only real business it draws in these days is for the casinos - which are doing quite spectacularly by comparison - and for... less savoury pursuits.
Outskirts
Only really developed into suburbs when Davenport started expanding, the development was short-lived. The projects came to a screeching halt when the money ran dry decades ago, and so half-finished houses are scattered throughout the regrown fields of grass. The remainder of what was cottage country extends across the northern half as well - a ghost town of creaking wood that's been dubbed the Witch's Hour and is frequented by college kids looking to haze each other. Those who live here come from a variety of backgrounds and socioeconomic statuses, but they all have one thing in common: a tendency to prefer being alone.
River's Edge
Filled to the brim with gated communities and McMansions, River's Edge may as well be part of another city. Taking up the entirety of Davenport's city bounds below the River, the streets are kept near-sparkling and every road is lined with great big, old trees kept trimmed to perfection. There are ample green spaces - about as well-kept as the rest of it - and trust me when I say, people will notice if you don't belong there. This is the home to the upper class and the wealthy, and very little else.

How Do I Apply?

Here on PG, we use profile applications. These profiles contain some basic info on your character, a freeform, and a shipper tab. All plotting takes part in Discord (DMs or the #plotting channel) or on-site personal messages, depending on your own personal preferences.

When it comes to filling out your application, all we ask is that you follow a few basic steps:

1. Make your accounts! If this is your first character, you'll need to make a member account and a character account. Otherwise, just the character account will do! Please note that for first-time members, we will be confirming you've got your member account set up. That means it should be made, it should have your member info filled out, and it should have non-default images! If any of your member info or images are missing on your member account, we will pend for it, just like if that stuff was missing on a character account.

2. Add in your avatar within User Controls > Edit Avatar Settings. This will size to be a square picture that usually won't show bigger than 60x60, however, we recommend it be at least 200x200 for sharpness on retina and 4K screens and it will crop and resize for you, so you honestly just need to upload a picture.

3. Fill out your profile's fields as best you can. Most fields are mandatory even if jCink won't stop you from saving if you leave them blank. When in doubt, read the description for each field - they'll say if it's optional.

4. Fill out the form in the get sorted thread and hit submit. This will prep all your claims codes for you and let staff know you need your profile looked at. You can also let us know you've posted a sorting request in the #finished-apps channel on the Discord if you like, and make sure to tag @Staff!

5. While you're waiting, you're welcome to work on any development stuff you like. This can include plotting, posting wanted ads, posting one-shots/journal entries/ship development threads, and requesting a dev board for other development work.

The introduction contains all the basic, short-field information we need to know about your character. None of this information should be too difficult!

  • quote — will format to all lowercase and cap at 300 characters
  • content warnings — should be thorough and be inclusive of all events in your character's life, discussed, implied, or planned so that people will know what plot points they could interact with when plotting with you
  • full name & nickname(s)
  • pronouns & gender

  • height & weight — as a note, if weight is a triggering subject for you, you can interact with this field in a way that suits you: using general terms, like 'average', or leaving it entirely blank. otherwise, there is a url for a calculator that can help you guesstimate a weight for your character

Context Fields

Context is for information specific to the world we've created in Play God. That means stuff like your character's powers (if they have any) and world-related information will be found here. It's a pretty short section, overall.

  • abilities / skills — if your character is a metahuman, this should be reflective of their abilities - which abilities do they have? how do they work? otherwise, list your human character's main skillsets - like if they're a talented linguist or have a knack for making the perfect chocolate chip cookies.
  • limitations / weaknesses — like the above, if your character is a metahuman, we're looking for limitations to their powers (specific ranges, lengths of time, ability strength, etc). if human, we want weaknesses - allergies, medical conditions, if they're just goddamn awful at math and please don't ask them to add more than two plus two... whatever comes to mind!
  • access to resources — where does your character land on the socioeconomic scale? are they struggling to eat, or have more money than they could ever use?
  • desire for socioeconomic change — how pissed are they about the systematic oppression of the poor and glorification of the rich?
  • community influence — how much influence do they have on their community? not necessarily the whole of davenport, though that can be a factor you consider
  • opinion on metahumans — do they have a positive, neutral, or negative view of metahumans and their existence?

Welcome to Davenport
Originally known for its rocky bluffs, its Acadian history, and being a quaint little town bordered by cottage country, Davenport's development boomed alongside that of Quantum Biomedical. Based out of a tiny warehouse in Davenport originally, when their success skyrocketed it brought an economic boom to the region and Davenport quickly became a thriving metropolitan city. When the economy crashed nation-wide decades later, the city fell into disrepair in most areas, relying heavily on tourism and donations from wealthy residents to survive. A shadow of what it once was, Davenport is no longer charming - though it maintains just enough of its prior charm to tempt tourists into visiting.

landmarks
Old Town

Previously full of charm, the porches and gables of Old Davenport have long since caved to time, weather, and lack of repair. Many of the houses have barely-patched leaks and shoddy wiring. It puts up appearances of being put-together in the areas that count - where the tourists still venture - but most of the area is rough around the edges, rather like the people living there. Home to the poor and the desperate, it's hardly safe to wander during the day, let alone at night. Keep an eye on your purse, lest you leave without its contents.

Casino Row

The best-kept part of Old Town, Casino Row is a series of casinos all hosted along one section of the main road. Made out of old French Colonial houses, each casino a couple of them connected by pathways, they are not as deceiving as you would think. They've largely been painted in gaudy colors and laden with flashing neon signs to draw the eye. It's kitschy, and frankly a little over-board, but it certainly manages to draw in the money.

Red Light District

Less well-kept than Casino Row, but at least without the leaks and the fire hazards (not good for business, you know!), the Red Light District is a mixture of brothels and row homes that are frequently lived in by families too large for them. Disease sweeps through on the regular - and not just because of the activities that are all-too-common here.

The Dockyards

Less tidy than the piers on the coastline, the docks are where real work gets done. Filled with cargo ships in the water and shipping containers on land, it's an absolute maze of a place that's easy enough to get lost in. It makes a fantastic place for criminals to conduct their business, but only during the off-hours. It's a little too busy otherwise.


Coastline

With the Old Town along part of the coast, the northern section and the southern strip of the coastline range have been split into two distinct sections in what is altogether considered the same region. The Southern strip is a mixture of tourist attractions, rental units, B&Bs, and whatever else Davenport can provide to encourage visitors to stop and to stay. The only particularly authentic thing about it is the daily market on the pier. The stands are shoddy, but the pier is immaculate - a newer construction intended for recreational vessels rather than trade. The occasional cruise ship still passes through here, but since the economic downfall, this is rare.

The northern coastline, on the other hand, is full of ramshackle cottages that have seen better days; remnants of when Davenport was a small summer vacationing spot, full of personal piers that are rotting away and secluded getaways, save the edge of Old Town where the old warehouses are. They haven't been used in a while, so it's informally known as The Graveyard to the locals. It's quite the den of thieves and provides decent cover for the homeless of the city.

Entertainment District

Crowded with bright colors, cheap tricks, and cheaper trinkets. Pickpockets and swindlers aplenty roam the streets here, looking to take advantage of unwary travelers. Everyone who works here drives a hard bargain, known for pricing up their wares so there's roam to haggle down to the price they really want to sell for - and pick up a tidy sum from those stupid enough not to haggle in the first place.

Bayside Market

A bustling hubbub of activity, even in the rainy weather, the market is the only living remnant of the time before the economic crash that has held up over the years and received regular maintenance. Filled with local artisans, bakers, chefs, and farmers who live near Davenport, the offerings change daily but it's always open from dawn until dusk.

The Graveyard

Sitting on the one remaining coastline piece that has not been worn into sand - or changed to it by the city - the Graveyard is a series of old warehouses. They used to house storage facilities and factories, but they were emptied and abandoned with the economic crash and have since become home to shady deals, the homeless, and those who don't want to be found for whatever reason. The police more or less leave this place alone, figuring that as long as the inhabitants don't venture out, it isn't worth the trouble.


Central Davenport

Fairing better than the majority of the city, Central Davenport keeps up appearances thanks to the money the university donates to put back into the neighborhood. The houses are neat and tidy, albeit some are a little on the small side, but they're safe and that's the important part of a city like this. Walking the streets alone in daylight without worry is nothing to snuff at.

University of Davenport

Bordering the second largest park in the city, the University of Davenport is best known for its biology and chemistry programs, which are run in partnership with Quantum Biomedical which still holds its primary location here in Davenport, in the lot next to the university campus. They feature many other programs as well, of course - they just aren't quite so illustrious. The streets around the campus are primarily filled with rental houses for students, or at the very least have turned their basements into guest suites for students to capitalize on the income.

Business Skyline

South of the university, near the river, is the business hub of Central Davenport. Skyscrapers tower over the rest of the city in a span of about 20 blocks from left to right and about 8 blocks from north to south. Beyond the office buildings, it's primarily just home to a whole host of chain restaurants and coffee shops, so it's also a decent place for a more legal meet-up - things like first dates and such.

River's Walk

Peering over the river into the lives of the rich is easiest done from the riverbank itself - which River's walk allows for easily. Kept up on the basis of it being visible from the southern side of the river, the boardwalk from the ocean to the city's edge is Davenport-famous for its spectacular sunsets, picturesque lookouts, and just generally being one of the nicest locations the city has to offer since before Quantum Biomedical existed.


River's Edge

While most of Davenport is dirty, crumbling, or at the very least showing its age - River's Edge decidedly is not. Wide streets, white sidewalks, and shining buildings with the sleek glass and straight edges of modern designs. Certainly, River's Edge is distinct and separate from the rest. The lots are large, the houses larger, and it's any wonder it's a part of Davenport at all. Only the elite can afford to live here - upper-level scientists of Quantum and those with family money, mostly.

Willow Creek Gated Community

The most illustrious of the gated communities in River's Edge, Willow Creek borders Centennial park and stretches to the shoreline of the river, which also makes it the largest of the communities. With stone walls, iron gates, and security posted at strategic parts throughout to ensure the resident's safety. It's not without its holes - but you'll have to work hard to find them.

Centennial Park

Free and open to everyone - even those who don't live in River's Edge - Centennial Park was initially landscaped by the charitable efforts of Quantum Biomedical to revitalize Davenport. It worked - for those that could afford it, anyway. The effort only pushed the gap between the rich and the poor further apart in the long run, spiking the land and housing prices around the Park and leading to River's Edge becoming as exclusive as it now is.

Government Sector

The buildings running along the eastern edge of the river are not quite as shiny as the rest. In excellent shape, they are practical brick and mortar and surrounded by parking lots and transit stations - the hub of government activity in Davenport. Although most of the local government offices are municipal services, such as police headquarters, there are a few small state and federal offices as well.


The Outskirts

Only part of the city per the city limit lines, really, the Outskirts are more nature than city. They started to be built up as suburbs just prior to the economic crash, but they weren't able to withstand the hardship and half-built houses, partly overtaken by nature, are scattered across fields of grass and cracked pavement. There are a few portions more built up, mostly close to where the Outskirts border other regions, but the majority of the land is country roads, sparse farms, and ghost towns.

Carnivale

On the corner of the Outskirts, Central Davenport, and the Coastline regions, Carnivale is an old amusement park turned permanent fairground when the money dried up. Skeletons of old rides are rusted brown, the paint peeling in large swatches, with the available activities are all centralized near the entrance to the park. The options now are relegated to simpler rides, like the carousel, a variety of fair games, and the nightly show of trick-trained animals, skilled persons, and - you guessed it - metahumans (formally declared "freaks" in the program).

Witch's Hour

Previously part of cottage country, these were the cabins further in from the Atlantic coast and centralized as a resort of sorts. The value of these properties dropped to near nothing and they were left abandoned when they wouldn't sell. All the old wood and decorations, mixed with dusty and destroyed personal effects that were left behind, give the place an eerie atmosphere and it's often used for hazing, or as a favorite exploration place of ghost hunters.

The Wilds

Spanning most of the Outskirts, the Wilds are nothing more than half-built, abandoned suburban communities, farms and their land, and rough unmaintained rural roads, mixed with actual untouched nature - usually in the form of forests and hiking trails. It's a beautiful area, if you're into that sort of thing, despite the lack of water that most people come to the area for.

Quantum Biomedical is a pharmaceutical company that found its original fame with a vaccine for a super-bug decades ago. It has flourished as a company since then, bringing Davenport with it for a while as its place of origin. When the global economy crashed, it took Quantum with it. Desperate for a way to keep the lights on, they threw everything they had into finding a new solution to a problem... even if that meant creating a problem themselves. Along the lines, they found the existence of metahumans and began delving into illegal experimentation. It's worked in bringing their finances back up - but at what cost?

The Pack started as a collection of survivors of Quantum Biomedical's metahuman experimentation a few years ago when they discovered that the testing facility had been rebuilt and swore to put a stop to Quantum's illegal activities. They've been quietly gathering intel and delaying Quantum's efforts as best they can over the years, but it's been altogether fruitless. Now that Quantum is playing with reality and turning humans into metas, they are ramping up their efforts. They have proof Quantum's experimentation and imprisonment of metas haven't actually stopped just because they got somewhere with their research, and they're determined to stop it.
